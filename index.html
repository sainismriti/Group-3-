Finding the Right College For You

With so many schools to choose from, we have streamlined the college application selection process. Studies show that amongst students applying to colleges, 73% see job placement as a very important factor. Likewise, 67% see cost of attendance as very important. It's clear that money is on many students minds.

To help students evaluate schools based on these concerns, our website will help you compare tuition costs, career outcomes, and social capital to help you choose a school that is affordable and likely to help you meet your career goals. Let`s get started.

Best Available Resources

The good news is that there are already a few great resources to help you understand which schools will statistically offer you the best opportunities.

College Scorecard

First, we recommend the College Scorecard's Compare Page from the U.S. Department of Education. Here you'll find a database that allows you to compare schools based on:

  • annual costs
  • graduation rates
  • typical earnings of graduates
  • average test scores
  • acceptance rate

    • Just search for the schools you're considering, select as many as you'd like, and hit compare!

    Social Capital Atlas

    Next, we recommend Opportunity Insights' Social Capital Atlas. By selecting 'college' under the 'view' section at the top of the page, you will now see a measurement of "economic connectedness" for every school in the U.S. This number tells you for students from low-income backgrounds, what percentage of their friends at this school come from high-income backgrounds. This may sound like a strange way to evaluate schools, but it's actually quite valuable. Social scientists have found that "social capital" or the economic strength of your community, plays a great role in your own upward income mobility. The greater the economic connectedness score shown at the link above, the more likely this college is to help you build you a strong network.

    College Salary Report

    If you're only concerned about future salary, we recommend payscale's College Salary Report. This site provides you with an easy to use page that allows you to search specific schools and compare expected salary 5 years after graduation (labeled "Early Career") as well as salaries 10 years after graduation (labeled "Mid-Career"). This offers a quick snapshot of how much money graduates of your schools of interest make throughout their career!
