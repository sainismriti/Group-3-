Choosing which schools to apply to isn't easy. With hundreds of schools to choose from and hundreds of websites with different metrics to evaluate schools by, we wanted to streamline the college application selection process.

What do students care about?

Studies show that the top five reasons students choose a college are:

  • Affordability
  • Degree programs offered
  • Reputation / academic quality
  • Career outcomes / job opportunities for graduates
  • Value of the education for the cost

    • Is there anything else you should consider?

    Other studies also that the greater connectedness there is between students of high-income and low-income backgrounds, the greater chance low income students have of upward mobility. This is sometimes referred to as "social capital" and there is yet another important measurement called "economic connectedness" that shows which schools are best at this.

    What we offer:

    There are many different websites and resources that together have all of this important information, but we want to put all of this in one place to make things easier. To help students evaluate schools based on these concerns, our website will help you compare the most important metrics between schools all in one place, so that you can narrow your list of schools to the ones that are most likely to help you meet your career goals.

    Our College Sorter will help you compare schools based on:

  • Admissions Rates for each school
  • ACT and SAT Scores to show where you're competitive
  • Size of the School by number of undergraduates
  • Graduation Rates to show how likely students are to leave with a degree
  • Debt for all graduates leaving the school
  • Salary two years after graduation
  • Economic Connectedness to tell you which schools offer the strongest social networks to improve your social capital
